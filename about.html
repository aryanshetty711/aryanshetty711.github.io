About Me

I'm a junior majoring in Computer Science. I have pursued this field because I love problem solving and programming is an enjoyable way for me to do just that. I first developed an interest in CS in high school when messing around with code found online. I find it immensely satisfying to automate a job and would rather spend hours programming the computer to do something that I could do myself in a fraction of the time. I am also minoring in economonics since I believe this field is integral to understanding how the world works.

I have been really engaged with the developer scene here at TAMU and as such I'm a Director at TAMUhack. I help with organizing our events - anything from workshops to the actual hackathons. Bringing people to collaborate on ideas is something that I found very rewarding. I really love working as part of a team and believe that I have gained the experience necessary to be a good team player.

I was an intern at Citrix technologies where I built an app that automates the generation of a bill of quantities from the system based off of user requirements. Working under mentors is something that I really seek because I believe it is the best way to learn in a professional setting. You can find my project in the portfolio section.

I am currently an undergraduate student researcher as well. My work consists of implementing deep learning techniques to develop an AI model that can detect actions and/or emotions of people from images and/or videos. It also entails designing new neural network architectures, and optimizing its performance. While this is something I have just started deep learning is a field in which I would really like to improve my skillset and I hope this project takes me to where I'd like to be.

I love watching MMA, movies, and am a keen follower of soccer (football!). I'm a huge fan of Manchester United. #GGMU
